# Frequently Asked Questions

## What is the EU AI Act Toolkit?

The EU AI Act Toolkit is a practical collection of templates, checklists, and documentation to support SMEs working toward readiness and compliance with the EU AI Act. It is not legal advice and does not provide compliance assurance.

## Is this toolkit legal advice?

No. The toolkit is educational and informational only. It is not legal advice, does not constitute legal counsel, and should not be treated as a substitute for review by qualified legal professionals. Always escalate sensitive questions to appropriate legal counsel.

## Who is this toolkit for?

The toolkit is designed for small and medium enterprises (SMEs), legal-tech professionals, compliance officers, and AI governance teams who need practical support for AI inventory, risk screening, vendor assessment, and internal documentation.

## Where should an SME start?

Start with the **Starter Pack**, especially:
- The 30-minute readiness self-assessment
- The one-page executive checklist
- The AI tool inventory starter template

Then review the sector pack relevant to your industry.

## What is the difference between the Starter Pack and Vendor Assessment Pack?

- **Starter Pack:** Readiness self-assessment, quick checklists, internal governance, and employee guidance. Use this first.
- **Vendor Assessment Pack:** AI vendor evaluation, procurement workflow, due diligence questionnaires, comparison matrices, and decision records. Use this when procuring or reviewing AI-enabled SaaS.

## Can this toolkit guarantee compliance?

No. The toolkit is a readiness and documentation support tool. It may help you prepare for compliance review, but it does not guarantee compliance. Always escalate final decisions to qualified legal and technical experts.

## Where should completed templates be stored?

Completed templates—especially those containing internal data, strategy, or confidential information—should be stored internally in secure, access-controlled systems (e.g., a private wiki, shared drive, or document management system). Do not commit completed templates to public GitHub repositories.

## What should not be posted in GitHub issues?

Do not share in public GitHub issues:
- Personal data (names, contact details, employee information)
- Customer data or contracts
- Confidential business information or strategy
- Security architecture or API keys
- Screenshots containing sensitive information

For security or privacy concerns, use [SECURITY.md](../SECURITY.md).

## When should a use case be escalated for legal or technical review?

Escalate to qualified experts when:
- The use case involves high-risk AI systems (as defined in the EU AI Act)
- Personal data processing is significant or sensitive
- Financial, health, or safety decisions depend on the AI system
- The vendor or deployment model is novel or unclear
- Internal review reveals gaps or unresolved risks

## How often should the toolkit be reviewed internally?

Review at least annually or after significant changes:
- When new AI tools are introduced
- When vendor relationships change
- When the EU AI Act or relevant guidance is updated
- When internal governance policies shift
- After any incident or near-miss involving AI systems

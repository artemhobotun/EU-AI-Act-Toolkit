Key Concepts

What the EU AI Act Covers

A brief plain-language overview of the regulation's scope — for context only. Always refer to the official text.

🏗️

Risk-Based Framework

The AI Act categorises AI systems by risk level: unacceptable risk (prohibited), high risk (strict requirements), limited risk (transparency), and minimal risk (general guidance).

⚠️

High-Risk AI Systems

High-risk systems include those used in employment, education, critical infrastructure, law enforcement, access to services, and certain biometric applications.

🚫

Prohibited Practices

Certain AI practices are prohibited entirely: social scoring by public authorities, real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces (with narrow exceptions), and manipulation of vulnerable groups.

🤖

General Purpose AI

The AI Act includes specific provisions for GPAI models (like large language models), with transparency and safety requirements for models of significant capability.

📅

Phased Application

The AI Act entered into force on 1 August 2024. Most obligations apply gradually — prohibited practices from early 2025, high-risk system rules from 2026, and full application by August 2027.

🔍

SME Considerations

The regulation includes provisions for SMEs and start-ups, including regulatory sandboxes and proportionate obligations where possible — though substantive compliance requirements still apply.

⚠️

Simplified overview only

The summary above is a simplified, non-exhaustive overview for orientation purposes. The official EUR-Lex text is the authoritative source. Consult a qualified legal professional for advice on your specific obligations.

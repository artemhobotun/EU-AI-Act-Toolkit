# Glossary

## AI system

A system that uses AI techniques to produce outputs such as predictions, recommendations, content, classifications, or decisions.

## AI-enabled tool

A regular business tool with AI features built in, such as summarising, drafting, ranking, or chat.

## Provider

The person or organization that develops, places on the market, or puts into service an AI system under its name or control.

## Deployer

The person or organization that uses an AI system in its own operations.

## General-purpose AI

An AI model or system that can be used for many different tasks rather than a single fixed purpose.

## Risk screening

A first-pass review to decide whether an AI use case is routine, sensitive, or should be escalated.

## Human oversight

A person reviewing, checking, or overriding AI output before it is relied on.

## AI literacy

Basic staff understanding of how AI tools work, where they can fail, and how to use them safely.

## Vendor questionnaire

A set of practical questions used to understand how a supplier handles data, logs, security, and transparency.

## Evidence pack

A simple set of documents and records that show how an AI use case was reviewed and managed.

## Incident log

A record of issues, near misses, and follow-up actions related to AI use.

## Source notes

Short references showing which official sources were checked when toolkit wording was created or updated.

## Escalation

Handing a use case, question, or risk to a person with more legal, security, privacy, or technical review responsibility.

## Personal data

Information about an identified or identifiable person.

## Special category data

Sensitive personal data that needs extra care under privacy law.

## SME readiness

Practical preparation for using, buying, or building AI tools with enough documentation and control to manage the risk.

# How to use this toolkit in a small company

This guide is a practical starting point for small teams. It helps you work through inventory, screening, and documentation without creating a heavy internal program on day one.

## Week 1: inventory

- list every AI tool, feature, pilot, and vendor service in use
- name an owner for each item
- note whether the use is internal, customer-facing, or both
- capture the vendor name, model name if known, and review date

## Week 2: risk screening

- complete a screening form for each item in the inventory
- flag uses that affect people, personal data, or sensitive decisions
- escalate anything involving employment, education, credit-like scoring, healthcare, biometric identification, or essential services
- record the first-pass view, not a final legal conclusion

## Week 3: vendor review

- ask vendors for documentation before buying or expanding a tool
- confirm whether customer data is used for training
- check available audit logs, security controls, and support channels
- document the limits of the vendor's product and the support they provide

## Week 4: internal policy and AI literacy

- publish a short internal AI use policy
- tell staff which tools are approved and which are not
- explain what data should never go into public tools
- record AI literacy onboarding and refresh training

## Monthly maintenance

- check for new tools, new pilots, and major vendor changes
- review incidents, near misses, and user feedback
- update the inventory when a new use case is added or retired

## Quarterly review

- review the inventory and screening notes
- re-check vendor documentation and support pages
- confirm internal policy, training records, and escalation paths are still current
- update source notes if official guidance changes

## What to escalate

- uses affecting employment, education, credit, healthcare, or essential services
- uses involving biometric identification or sensitive personal data
- customer-facing outputs that can materially mislead or harm users
- vendor situations where transparency, logs, or data handling are unclear
- anything that looks like a higher-risk use case in your sector

## What not to do

- do not rely on a tool without understanding what it does with data
- do not store secrets, passwords, or confidential business information in unapproved tools
- do not treat AI output as final advice without human review
- do not claim compliance based on a template alone

## Where to store evidence

Keep your working evidence in a simple folder or workspace that is easy to review:

- AI inventory
- screening forms
- vendor questionnaires
- policy and training records
- incident log
- source notes
- decision logs and approvals

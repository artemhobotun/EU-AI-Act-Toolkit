# Versioning and Maintenance Policy

## Purpose

This toolkit is versioned to help users understand when templates, checklists, and source notes changed.

## Versioning approach

- Patch updates: wording, typo, link, formatting, minor template improvements
- Minor updates: new templates, new checklists, new use-case examples, new workflow docs
- Major updates: significant restructuring or major legal/source changes

## Review cadence

- lightweight monthly review
- quarterly source review
- ad hoc review after official guidance, standards, or major legal updates

## What triggers an update

- official EU guidance
- source note change
- new implementation materials
- practical feedback from users
- template usability improvements
- issue or PR from a contributor

## Release checklist

- quality script passes
- disclaimer still visible
- source notes reviewed if legal wording changed
- templates remain SME-friendly
- no confidential data added
- changelog updated
- release notes updated

## Avoiding legal overclaiming

- do not say "provides compliance assurance"
- use "readiness", "screening", and "starting point"
- escalate uncertain use cases for legal review

# What to Escalate for Legal Review

This guide helps SMEs identify situations where this toolkit should not be used alone.

These situations may require deeper review, should be escalated, and should not rely on this toolkit alone.

## Use cases affecting people

Examples:

- decisions about access, ranking, eligibility, monitoring, scoring, selection, or prioritisation

## Employment and worker management

Examples:

- candidate screening
- employee monitoring
- performance scoring
- scheduling or termination-related tools

## Education or training decisions

Examples:

- admission
- assessment
- scoring
- progress tracking

## Credit, insurance, or financial access

Examples:

- scoring
- eligibility
- pricing
- risk ranking

## Healthcare, welfare, or essential services

Examples:

- triage support
- benefit access
- essential service prioritisation

## Biometric or sensitive uses

Examples:

- biometric identification
- emotion recognition
- sensitive category inference

## Public authority, law enforcement, migration, or border contexts

These contexts should be escalated and not handled through a simple SME checklist.

## Large-scale personal data processing

Privacy, data protection, and security review may be needed.

## Vendor uncertainty

Escalate if:

- vendor cannot explain AI functionality
- vendor cannot provide documentation
- vendor uses customer data for training without clarity
- vendor cannot explain logs, security, or human oversight support

## No human oversight

Escalate if:

- outputs are used automatically
- staff cannot challenge the system
- no review process exists

## What to collect before asking for advice

- system description
- vendor documentation
- intended use
- affected people
- data categories
- outputs
- decision impact
- human oversight
- screenshots or workflow notes
- contracts or DPA if available

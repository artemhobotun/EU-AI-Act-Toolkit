# Use case: AI-enabled CRM scoring

## Scenario

A company uses AI inside a CRM to score leads, rank opportunities, or prioritize customer outreach.

## Why SMEs use it

It can help sales teams focus their time on higher-priority contacts.

## Typical data involved

- customer profiles
- engagement data
- sales notes
- account history
- potentially personal data

## Questions to ask

- Does the score affect decisions about people or access to services?
- Is the scoring explanation available to users?
- Is there human review before action?
- Is customer data used for training?

## Basic risk screening notes

CRM scoring can look routine, but it may still affect people through prioritization, access, or business decisions. If the scoring has employment, credit-like, or customer-impact effects, escalate for deeper review.

## Documentation to keep

- inventory entry
- screening form
- vendor questionnaire
- human review notes
- decision log for the scoring use case

## Human review

Human review should be used before any important action is taken solely from the score.

## Vendor questions

- What AI functionality is included?
- What documentation explains the scoring logic?
- What logs are available?
- What support is provided for EU AI Act compliance?

## Practical next step

Write down exactly how the score may be used and who can override it.

# Use case: Internal ChatGPT use

## Scenario

A small company allows staff to use ChatGPT or a similar tool for drafting emails, summarizing notes, and brainstorming internal work.

## Why SMEs use it

It can save time on first drafts, summaries, and routine writing tasks.

## Typical data involved

- internal notes
- meeting summaries
- draft text
- occasionally personal data if staff are not careful

## Questions to ask

- Is this tool approved for work use?
- What data must never be entered?
- Are staff expected to review outputs before use?
- Does the vendor use customer data for training?

## Basic risk screening notes

This is usually an internal productivity use case, but it still needs data rules, human review, and incident reporting. If staff use it with sensitive data, it needs more care.

## Documentation to keep

- inventory entry
- internal AI use policy
- AI literacy record
- vendor questionnaire if the tool is paid or centrally managed
- incident log if anything goes wrong

## Human review

Human review should happen before anything external is sent or any important decision is made based on the output.

## Vendor questions

- What AI functionality is included?
- Is customer input used for training?
- What logs or admin controls are available?
- What support is provided for policy enforcement?

## Practical next step

Put the tool in the inventory and give staff a short approved-use note.

## Disclaimer

This is a readiness example only. It is not legal advice and does not provide compliance assurance.

# Use case: Customer support chatbot

## Scenario

A company uses a chatbot on its website or help center to answer common customer questions.

## Why SMEs use it

It can reduce repetitive support requests and improve response speed.

## Typical data involved

- customer messages
- account or order references
- support history
- potentially personal data

## Questions to ask

- Does the bot give advice or only routine support information?
- Is there a clear human handoff?
- What happens when the bot is unsure?
- Is customer data used for vendor training?

## Basic risk screening notes

Customer-facing bots should be screened carefully because they can mislead users, disclose sensitive information, or create support errors. Clear human review and escalation are important.

## Documentation to keep

- inventory entry
- screening form
- vendor questionnaire
- support workflow showing human handoff
- incident log for bad responses or leakage

## Human review

Human review should be available for sensitive requests, complaints, account issues, or anything the bot cannot answer confidently.

## Vendor questions

- What AI functionality is included?
- Is the system GPAI-based?
- What audit logs exist?
- What transparency information is provided?
- What support is provided for EU AI Act compliance?

## Practical next step

Limit the bot to clearly defined support topics and add a human escalation route.

# Use case: Marketing content generation

## Scenario

A small team uses AI to draft social posts, web copy, newsletters, and campaign ideas.

## Why SMEs use it

It speeds up content creation and helps smaller teams ship more consistently.

## Typical data involved

- brand guidelines
- draft copy
- campaign notes
- product information
- sometimes customer data if teams are careless

## Questions to ask

- Is the content reviewed before publishing?
- Are restricted topics or claims blocked?
- Do staff avoid entering confidential information?
- Are image or text outputs checked for rights and accuracy?

## Basic risk screening notes

This is usually a lower-risk internal use case, but it still needs human review and content controls. Claims and customer-facing statements should be checked carefully.

## Documentation to keep

- inventory entry
- internal AI use policy
- AI literacy record
- approval checklist for public content
- incident log if something goes wrong

## Human review

Human review should happen before anything customer-facing is published.

## Vendor questions

- What AI functionality is included?
- Are outputs stored or used for training?
- What controls exist for brand safety and access management?
- What logs or audit trails are available?

## Practical next step

Allow the tool only for draft work and keep human approval mandatory for publication.

# Use case: Vendor AI tool procurement

## Scenario

A company is evaluating a new AI-enabled SaaS tool or model API for internal or customer use.

## Why SMEs use it

It helps compare vendors and avoid buying tools with unclear data handling or poor controls.

## Typical data involved

- procurement notes
- vendor documentation
- product descriptions
- security and privacy responses
- contract and DPA references

## Questions to ask

- What AI functionality is included?
- Is the system GPAI-based?
- What documentation is available?
- What data is used for training or inference?
- Are customer inputs used for training?
- What security controls exist?
- What audit logs exist?
- What transparency information is provided?
- What support is provided for EU AI Act compliance?

## Basic risk screening notes

Procurement is where readiness work should start. If the vendor is unclear about training, logs, transparency, or data handling, escalate before purchase.

## Documentation to keep

- vendor questionnaire
- procurement checklist
- decision log
- approved-use note or exception record
- source notes if a legal or guidance question comes up

## Human review

Human review should happen before purchase approval and before any expanded deployment.

## Vendor questions

- What AI functionality is included?
- What audit logs exist?
- What support is provided for EU AI Act compliance?
- What happens if model behavior changes?

## Practical next step

Do not buy first and ask questions later. Capture vendor answers before approval.

## Disclaimer

This is a readiness example only. It is not legal advice and does not provide compliance assurance.

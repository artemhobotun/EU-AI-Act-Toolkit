# Use case: Document review assistant

## Scenario

A company uses AI to summarize, compare, or extract points from contracts, policies, or other business documents.

## Why SMEs use it

It helps teams scan large documents faster and prepare first drafts or summaries.

## Typical data involved

- business documents
- contracts
- policies
- potentially confidential information

## Questions to ask

- Is the tool allowed to process confidential documents?
- Are outputs always reviewed by a human?
- Are documents retained by the vendor?
- Can data be deleted or excluded from training?

## Basic risk screening notes

This use case often touches confidential or sensitive business material. It needs strong data rules and human review, especially where the output may influence legal, commercial, or HR decisions.

## Documentation to keep

- inventory entry
- internal AI use policy
- vendor questionnaire
- document-handling rules
- incident log

## Human review

Human review should happen before using the output for legal, commercial, or operational decisions.

## Vendor questions

- What AI functionality is included?
- What data is used for training or inference?
- Are customer inputs used for training?
- What security controls and logs exist?
- What support is provided for EU AI Act compliance?

## Practical next step

Limit the tool to draft and summary support, and keep confidential document handling explicit.

## Disclaimer

This is a readiness example only. It is not legal advice and does not provide compliance assurance.

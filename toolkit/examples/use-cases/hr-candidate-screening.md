# Use case: HR candidate screening

## Scenario

A company uses AI to rank or filter job applicants before a human review stage.

## Why SMEs use it

It may seem faster for large applicant pools and early-stage sorting.

## Typical data involved

- CVs
- cover letters
- interview notes
- employment history
- personal data

## Questions to ask

- Does the tool influence hiring decisions?
- Is a human reviewer always in the loop?
- Can applicants be excluded automatically?
- Does the vendor explain how scores are produced?

## Basic risk screening notes

This use case affects people and employment decisions. It should be escalated for deeper review. It may need stricter controls, documentation, and internal approval than a standard productivity tool.

## Documentation to keep

- inventory entry
- screening form
- decision log
- vendor questionnaire
- human review notes
- incident log

## Human review

Human review should be mandatory before any hiring decision is made. The tool should not be treated as the final decision-maker.

## Vendor questions

- What AI functionality is included?
- What documentation explains limitations and bias controls?
- What transparency information is provided?
- What audit logs and export options exist?
- What support is provided for EU AI Act compliance?

## Practical next step

Pause rollout until legal, HR, and privacy review the use case and document the oversight model.

## Disclaimer

This is a readiness example only. It is not legal advice and does not provide compliance assurance.

# Decision Status Guide

## Approved

Meaning: the vendor and use case can proceed.
Use when: risks are understood and acceptable.
Evidence to keep: questionnaire, documents, decision record, review date.

## Approved with conditions

Meaning: the vendor can proceed only with specific controls.
Use when: mitigations are needed before rollout.
Evidence to keep: conditions, owner, follow-up actions, review date.

## Rejected

Meaning: the vendor should not be used.
Use when: risks are too high or information is missing.
Evidence to keep: decision record and reason.

## Escalated

Meaning: the case needs legal, privacy, security, or management review.
Use when: the use case affects people, data, or sensitive contexts.
Evidence to keep: escalation note, supporting documents, next step.

## Deferred

Meaning: the review is paused.
Use when: more information is needed.
Evidence to keep: open questions and follow-up date.

## Pilot only

Meaning: the tool may be tested in a limited setting.
Use when: wider rollout is not yet approved.
Evidence to keep: pilot scope, controls, review notes, exit criteria.

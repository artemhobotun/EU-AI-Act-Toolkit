# Vendor Red Flags Checklist

Use this checklist to spot vendor issues that may need escalation.

- Vendor cannot clearly explain AI features
  - Why it matters: you cannot screen the tool properly.
  - Suggested next step: request a plain-language product explanation.
- Vendor cannot explain whether customer data is used for training
  - Why it matters: data use may affect privacy and procurement decisions.
  - Suggested next step: ask for a written answer and contract language.
- No DPA where personal data is processed
  - Why it matters: personal data processing may need formal terms.
  - Suggested next step: pause approval until privacy review is complete.
- No subprocessor information
  - Why it matters: you may not know who handles your data.
  - Suggested next step: request the subprocessor list and update notice process.
- No security documentation
  - Why it matters: you cannot judge basic risk.
  - Suggested next step: request security overview and admin control summary.
- No retention or deletion information
  - Why it matters: data may stay longer than expected.
  - Suggested next step: ask for retention and deletion rules.
- No audit logs
  - Why it matters: you may not be able to review or investigate activity.
  - Suggested next step: ask for log access or export options.
- No human oversight controls
  - Why it matters: outputs may be used without review.
  - Suggested next step: require human review or escalate the use case.
- Marketing claims without documentation
  - Why it matters: claims may not be operationally useful.
  - Suggested next step: ask for documents, not slogans.
- Vendor refuses documentation
  - Why it matters: you cannot complete a basic review.
  - Suggested next step: escalate or reject.
- AI tool used in a sensitive or high-impact context without deeper review
  - Why it matters: the use case may need more than a lightweight checklist.
  - Suggested next step: escalate legal, privacy, or security review.
- No clear escalation/support process
  - Why it matters: problems may not be handled in time.
  - Suggested next step: ask how incidents and complaints are handled.

# Vendor Scoring Worksheet

A lightweight worksheet to compare and evaluate AI-enabled SaaS vendors before purchase or renewal.

## Purpose

This worksheet helps you:
- Compare multiple vendors on key readiness criteria
- Document procurement decisions
- Support internal governance discussions
- Identify areas needing escalation or specialist review

## How to use

1. List vendors and products you are evaluating
2. For each criterion, score 1–5 based on the vendor's documentation and responses
3. Calculate the average score
4. Document open questions and risk level
5. Use the result to inform your decision and escalation

## Scoring scale

- **1** = Weak, unclear, or insufficient evidence
- **2** = Partial, some gaps
- **3** = Acceptable for preliminary review
- **4** = Strong and adequately documented
- **5** = Strong and comprehensive

## Criteria

| Criterion | What to assess |
|---|---|
| Documentation quality | Does the vendor provide clear, organized documentation? |
| Data use clarity | Are customer data flows clearly explained? |
| Training clarity | Is training on customer data handling clear and available? |
| Human oversight support | Does the tool support human review and override? |
| Audit logs | Are audit logs available for compliance review? |
| Security documentation | Are security practices and certifications documented? |
| DPA/subprocessor | Is a Data Processing Agreement provided with clear subprocessor information? |
| EU AI Act readiness | Does the vendor support EU AI Act compliance discussion? |

## What not to overinterpret

- This worksheet supports readiness and documentation review, not legal advice
- A high score does not guarantee compliance
- A low score does not mean the tool cannot be used—it means escalation or specialist review is needed
- Use professional judgment alongside scoring

## When to escalate

Escalate to legal or technical specialists when:
- Risk level is flagged as "High"
- Average score is below 3.0
- Multiple open questions remain unanswered
- The vendor's data processing model is novel or unclear
- The use case involves sensitive data or high-risk AI systems

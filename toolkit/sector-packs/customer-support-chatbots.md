# Sector pack: Customer support chatbots

## Typical SME use cases

- FAQ chatbot
- website support chatbot
- ticket triage
- support reply drafting

## Common tools

- customer support bots in SaaS platforms
- website chat widgets
- support copilot tools
- ticketing add-ons

## Typical data involved

- customer messages
- account or order references
- support logs
- personal data
- complaint history

## Key readiness questions

- Does the bot hand off to a human when needed?
- What data is kept in logs?
- Does the vendor use customer inputs for training?
- Is the bot transparent about limitations?
- Are response templates reviewed before use?

## Basic risk screening notes

Customer support bots are customer-facing and can create misleading answers, privacy issues, or escalation failures. They should be screened carefully and often need stronger documentation.

## Documentation to keep

- AI inventory entry
- risk screening form
- vendor questionnaire
- support escalation workflow
- human review notes
- incident log

## Internal policy controls

- approved topics
- forbidden topics
- human handoff rules
- logging and retention settings
- customer-facing transparency notice if appropriate

## Human review expectations

Human review should be available for complaints, sensitive requests, account issues, and anything the bot cannot answer confidently.

## When to escalate

- the bot can affect rights, access, or service outcomes
- logs include personal data or sensitive information
- the vendor cannot explain training data, retention, or audit logs
- the bot is used for more than routine FAQ handling

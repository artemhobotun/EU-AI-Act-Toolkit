# Sector pack: Internal productivity and generative AI

## Typical SME use cases

- drafting internal emails or notes
- summarising meetings and long documents
- translation and rewriting
- internal research and brainstorming
- generating first drafts for policy, marketing, or operations work

## Common tools

- ChatGPT
- Claude
- Microsoft Copilot
- similar internal assistant tools

## Typical data involved

- meeting notes
- internal drafts
- policy text
- operational context
- occasionally personal or confidential data if staff are not careful

## Key readiness questions

- Is the tool approved for work use?
- What data must never be entered?
- Is human review required before the output is used?
- Do staff know how to report unsafe or wrong outputs?
- Is the vendor using customer data for training?

## Basic risk screening notes

This is usually a lower-attention use case when limited to internal drafting and summarising. It becomes more sensitive if staff enter confidential, personal, or regulated data.

## Documentation to keep

- AI inventory entry
- internal AI use policy
- AI literacy record
- vendor questionnaire if centrally managed
- incident log for any leakage or unsafe output

## Internal policy controls

- approved tools list
- do-not-enter data rules
- human review before external use
- escalation contact

## Human review expectations

Human review should happen before anything external is sent, published, or relied on for an important decision.

## When to escalate

- staff are using unapproved tools with sensitive data
- outputs affect customer, legal, HR, or financial decisions
- the tool is connected to automation or external sending
- the vendor cannot explain training, logs, or retention

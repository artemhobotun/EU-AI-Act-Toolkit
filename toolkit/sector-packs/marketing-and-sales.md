# Sector pack: Marketing and sales

## Typical SME use cases

- content generation
- ad copy drafting
- customer segmentation
- lead scoring
- CRM scoring

## Common tools

- marketing assistants
- CRM scoring add-ons
- email and campaign drafting tools

## Typical data involved

- customer lists
- campaign performance data
- CRM records
- personal data
- behavioural or engagement data

## Key readiness questions

- Is the tool only drafting content or also prioritising people?
- Are scores used to make decisions about access or treatment?
- Is human review required before publishing or actioning outputs?
- Does the vendor use customer data for training?
- Are outputs checked for misleading claims?

## Basic risk screening notes

Scoring or prioritisation affecting people may require deeper review, especially when personal data is involved.

## Documentation to keep

- AI inventory entry
- screening form
- vendor questionnaire
- content approval workflow
- human review notes
- incident log

## Internal policy controls

- approved content review path
- rules for personal data use
- rules for claims, pricing, and promotions
- escalation for scoring and prioritisation

## Human review expectations

Human review should happen before customer-facing publication and before any score is used for important decisions.

## When to escalate

- scoring affects access, pricing, or treatment
- the vendor cannot explain data use or logs
- the tool uses customer data for training without clarity
- the use case moves beyond draft support into automated prioritisation
